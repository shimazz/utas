CASTESD2018

Participating in Symposium:

  1. Attendance only
  2. Poster presentation (THREE students only are allowed to do presentation).

About Sur CAS

Sur College of Applied Sciences

College of Applied Sciences Sur has pioneered many changes in the community of Sur and its surrounding areas especially in the field of education and community development. The Campus initially began in 1987 as a middle level college that offered Diploma in Education, subsequently was taken over by the Ministry of Higher Education in 1995 as a full-fledged College of Education offering the Degree of Bachelors in Education to many of its enterprising students.

Later on in 2005-2006, the College was further transformed into the College of Applied Sciences further enhancing its capabilities by offering practical industry driven majors such as Mass Communication, Information Technology and Applied Bio-Technology With 945 students attending from different regions in and around Sur, the dedicated staff is committed to ensure the overall development of both the students as well as the up-gradation of the curriculum periodically. This redeeming quality provides the student community with opportunities to engage with others from different backgrounds.

The College is nationally recognized as a front-runner in creating innovative approaches that fosters the success of students from all majors and focuses on efforts to make the College a leader in undergraduate student success and emphasizes on improving graduate education.

The College has a diverse and multi -cultural staff carefully chosen from across the globe to facilitate an understanding of the global aspects required as per the majors. Constant interaction with the requirement of the industry and academia emphasizes the College’s commitment to addressing issues and problems confronting Sur, and establishing it as a priority in the institution's initiatives.

Professor Saif Al Bahry Bio

Professor Saif Al-Bahry is a member the Department of Biology, College of Science, SQU. He was a Head of the Department and later became a Dean of the College. Currently, He is a Director of Oil and Gas Research Center, SQU. He was a Co-Chair of the Academic Council of the new University of Oman. He served in a Shura Council committee to revise regulations related to sewage effluents. He is a member of the Oman Animal and Plant Genetic Resources Center (OAPGRC) Scientific Committees of the Research Council. He is a board of directors of the Oman Center of Marine Biotechnology (OCMB) member and a chair of the OCMB technical committee. During his deanship, he has established a high-end Central Applied and Analytical Research Unit (CAARU). He was involved in the establishment of an undergraduate biotechnology degree, 1997, MSc and PhD programs in the Department of Biology, College of Science. He was enrolled in setting up the new applied biotechnology program in Sur College of Applied Sciences since 2008. His is a multidisciplinary researcher with more than 280 publications in journals, proceedings, chapters in books, technical reports and conference abstracts. His research interests and experiences in biotechnology include food and water safety, spread of antibiotic-resistant-bacteria to environment, and heavy metals pollution, stress proteins, microbial genomics, bioenergy, crude oil recovery, bioremediation and crude oil souring and mitigation. He has advised many graduate students and projects

Submission

CASTESD 18 invites original unpublished research work in the following categories:

  1. Full paper submission: Original unpublished research articles are invited for publication in the journals listed under publication partners. The full paper can be adapted for conference presentation (15 minutes plus 5 minutes for questions). Full length articles must adhere to the template and should not exceed six pages inclusive of references. They will be accepted or rejected on the basis of recommendations by carefully selected reviewers.
  2. Abstract only submission: Authors may, if they wish, present at the conference only (no publication of a full paper). In this case, they need only submit an abstract (500 words), using the CASTESD 2018 template.
  3. Poster presentation (students only): Students can present their research via poster presentation. Poster proposals should follow the CASTESD 2018 guidelines and should be submitted online for review.

All submitted papers (including abstract and poster) will undergo plagiarism detection and double-blind review; meaning author and reviewer identities are concealed from both ends. Authors are advised not to leave any trace which discloses their identity in the body of paper at this stage. Review criteria includes originality, significance, language used, and current.
